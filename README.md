Title: The opening showed great potential, but the rest of the story did not rise to that level.
Date: Reviewed in the United States on April 12, 2022
Review: The opening was incredible, Sage showed huge promise, and then actions misfired, things got clumsy, and it never rose to the level of the opening.

Too much of Sage was "as seen from the outside" while novels allow the writer to open up the characters and let us access their heart and mind. Here, no more than skimming the surface took place. Almost like a fishing lure, things happened to tempt us to enter the story, but it never did really open the door to that.

Magic/No Magic interactions were too much playing it careful and not enough of the emotion of the mind and the soul. That hurt me, much like being promised a treat and then being given something bland.

It was good-- but it never went deep enough to be great. It was almost as if the story was forced to stay in G ratings, and the dramatics were PG ratings, which makes it safe but also keeps it from really rising to the level of drama.

It felt like a good writer being too cautious, so I admit that was there was good, but only good, not very good, not great, just good.

If the romance element is allowed to rise, the way dough is allowed to rise before baking it into bread, then the characters have the potential to become great.

It is a good start, but only finished as "kinda okay" by playing it too safe. That's why the three out of five rating. I hope it does not play safe, but takes risks-- structurally and romantically and "environment occupation" have a huge playing field.

It would be a shame to ignore the potential.
